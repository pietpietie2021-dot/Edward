ZipDrop is an on-demand roadside assistance platform that connects you — whether you're a driver in need or a qualified mechanic looking for work — in real time. When your vehicle breaks down, ZipDrop's matching engine pinpoints your exact location, identifies the most suitable available mechanic nearby, and dispatches them directly to you. No phone trees, no lengthy hold times, no uncertainty about who's coming or when. From the moment you submit a request, ZipDrop handles the coordination so you can stay safe and get moving again as quickly as possible.

## The two sides of ZipDrop

ZipDrop brings together two distinct groups of people on a single platform.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Drivers" icon="car">
    Drivers are anyone who needs roadside assistance when their vehicle breaks down, won't start, runs out of fuel, suffers a flat tyre, or experiences any other mechanical issue. You open the app, describe your problem, and ZipDrop takes care of finding the right help. You don't need any technical knowledge — just your location and a brief description of what's wrong.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Mechanics" icon="wrench">
    Mechanics are qualified automotive professionals who use ZipDrop to find and accept nearby breakdown jobs. They travel to the driver's location with the tools and parts needed to diagnose and fix the problem on the spot. Mechanics set their own availability and choose which jobs to accept, working flexibly around their existing commitments.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## How job matching works

When you submit a breakdown request, ZipDrop's matching algorithm gets to work immediately. Here's what happens behind the scenes:

1. **You share your location and job type.** The app uses your device's GPS to pinpoint your exact position. You then select the category that best describes your problem — for example, flat tyre, battery jump-start, fuel delivery, engine fault, or lockout. Adding a short note about your specific situation helps the matched mechanic arrive prepared.

2. **ZipDrop identifies the best available mechanics.** The algorithm searches for mechanics who are active, unassigned, and within a serviceable radius of your location. Candidates are ranked by a combination of three factors: proximity to you, their overall rating from previous jobs, and how closely their listed specialisms match your job type. A tyre specialist, for example, will rank higher for a flat tyre job than a generalist at the same distance.

3. **Mechanics have 30 seconds to accept.** The top-ranked mechanic receives your request as an alert. If they accept within 30 seconds, they're assigned to your job. If they decline or don't respond in time, the request automatically passes to the next mechanic on the ranked list. This continues until someone accepts.

4. **You see who's coming.** Once a mechanic accepts, you immediately see their name, profile photo, star rating, and estimated arrival time. You can track their progress in real time on the in-app map, so you always know exactly how far away they are.

## The job lifecycle

Every ZipDrop job follows a clear, structured flow from first tap to final rating.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Driver requests help">
    You open the ZipDrop app, allow location access, choose your job type, add any relevant details, and confirm your request. ZipDrop immediately begins searching for available mechanics.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Mechanic matched and accepts">
    ZipDrop's algorithm identifies the best-matched mechanic and sends them your job alert. Once they accept, you receive a notification confirming who is on their way, along with their ETA and contact options.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Mechanic navigates to you">
    The mechanic uses in-app navigation to travel to your exact location. You can watch their progress on the map in real time and message or call them through the app if you need to share additional details — such as a car park level, motorway junction, or access gate code.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Mechanic arrives and starts work">
    When the mechanic reaches you, they mark the job as started in the app. You can verify their identity by checking their name and photo in the app before they begin. The mechanic then diagnoses the issue and carries out the agreed repair or assistance.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Job completed, driver confirms">
    Once the work is done, the mechanic marks the job as complete. You review a summary of the work carried out and confirm completion in the app. If there are any concerns, you can flag them at this stage before payment is taken.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Payment processed, both parties rate each other">
    Payment is automatically charged to your saved payment method — no cash or card machines needed. You and the mechanic are then each prompted to leave a star rating and optional written review. These ratings feed directly into the matching algorithm to improve future jobs for everyone.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Quality assurance

ZipDrop holds every mechanic on the platform to a high standard before they ever accept their first job — and continuously throughout their time on the platform.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Vetting before activation" icon="shield-check">
    Every mechanic completes an enhanced background check, identity verification, and a review of their professional qualifications before their account is activated. Full details of the vetting process are covered in the [Trust & Safety](/platform/trust-and-safety) guide.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Continuous rating monitoring" icon="star">
    After every completed job, both you and the mechanic rate each other. ZipDrop's team monitors rating trends across the platform. Mechanics whose ratings fall below the required threshold are suspended pending review, ensuring only high-quality professionals remain active.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Tip>
  If a mechanic's behaviour during a job falls short of your expectations, use the in-app reporting tool immediately after the job ends. Your feedback is reviewed by ZipDrop's Trust & Safety team and helps keep the platform safe and reliable for everyone.
</Tip>

<Note>
  ZipDrop does not employ mechanics directly. All mechanics on the platform are independent service providers who have agreed to ZipDrop's terms of service and quality standards. They operate their own businesses and are responsible for their own equipment, tools, and insurance. ZipDrop acts as the technology platform that connects drivers and mechanics.
</Note>

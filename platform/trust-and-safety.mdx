Your safety is the foundation everything at ZipDrop is built on. Breaking down is already stressful enough — the last thing you need is uncertainty about who is turning up to help you. That's why ZipDrop invests heavily in vetting every mechanic before they appear on the platform, building in-app tools that protect both you and the mechanic throughout every job, and maintaining a 24/7 support team ready to act if anything goes wrong. This page explains exactly what ZipDrop does to keep every interaction safe, trustworthy, and transparent.

## Mechanic vetting

Every mechanic goes through a rigorous verification process before their ZipDrop account is activated. No mechanic can accept a job until all of the following checks are complete and approved.

<Accordion title="Enhanced DBS background check">
  All mechanics must complete an Enhanced Disclosure and Barring Service (DBS) check, the highest level available in England and Wales. This check covers criminal records, cautions, and relevant barred list information. ZipDrop reviews the result and reserves the right to decline any applicant based on the findings. Equivalent checks are carried out for mechanics based in Scotland (PVG scheme) and Wales.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Government-issued ID verification">
  Every mechanic must verify their identity using a valid, government-issued photo ID — such as a UK passport or driving licence. ZipDrop uses a third-party identity verification service to authenticate documents and confirm that the person submitting the application matches the ID provided. This ensures the person arriving at your breakdown is the same person ZipDrop has vetted.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Right to work check (UK)">
  ZipDrop confirms that every mechanic has the legal right to work in the United Kingdom before their account is approved. Required documentation is collected and verified in line with UK Home Office guidance. This check is repeated if a mechanic's visa or work status changes.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Professional qualifications review">
  Mechanics are required to provide evidence of relevant automotive qualifications or demonstrable professional experience. ZipDrop's onboarding team reviews submitted credentials and may request additional documentation or references before approval. Mechanics can list specific specialisms — such as EVs, diesel engines, or tyre fitting — only after providing evidence to support that claim.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Vehicle insurance verification">
  Every mechanic must hold a valid commercial motor insurance policy that covers them for work-related driving. Personal car insurance is not accepted. ZipDrop verifies the policy details directly and checks renewal status periodically. A mechanic whose insurance lapses is automatically suspended until they provide updated documentation.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Ongoing rating monitoring">
  Once a mechanic is active, ZipDrop monitors their ratings continuously. After every completed job, you rate the mechanic on a five-star scale. ZipDrop's Trust & Safety team reviews rating trends, and any mechanic whose average falls below the minimum threshold is suspended from the platform pending a review. Mechanics can also be removed immediately if they receive reports of unsafe or unprofessional behaviour.
</Accordion>

## Privacy protections

ZipDrop is designed so that you never have to share sensitive personal information with the mechanic — or vice versa.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Masked phone numbers" icon="phone">
    Your personal phone number is never shared with the mechanic, and theirs is never shared with you. All calls and messages between drivers and mechanics go through ZipDrop's anonymised communication layer. If the job ends or is cancelled, the connection is closed and neither party can contact the other through ZipDrop.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Location data limits" icon="location-dot">
    Your precise location is only shared with a mechanic once they have accepted your job. Before acceptance, mechanics see only a general area. Once the job is marked as complete, location sharing stops automatically. ZipDrop does not use your location data for advertising or share it with third parties.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Encrypted payment details" icon="lock">
    Your payment information is encrypted and stored securely by ZipDrop's payment processor. Mechanics never see your card details, bank information, or any other financial data. You are charged automatically once you confirm job completion — no payment information is ever exchanged in person.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Data retention controls" icon="database">
    You can request a copy of your personal data or ask for your account to be deleted at any time through the app under **Settings → Privacy**. ZipDrop retains data only as long as required by law or legitimate business need, in accordance with UK GDPR.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## In-app safety features

ZipDrop includes a set of built-in safety tools that are available to you on every single job, whether you are a driver or a mechanic.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Emergency SOS" icon="siren">
    A single tap on the SOS button in the app dials 999 and shares your live GPS coordinates with emergency services. Use this if you are in immediate physical danger. The button is always visible on the active job screen — you never have to navigate away from the map to find it.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Trip sharing" icon="share-nodes">
    Before or during a job, you can share a live tracking link with anyone you trust — a friend, family member, or colleague. They can follow the mechanic's progress to your location and see your position in real time, without needing to download the ZipDrop app themselves.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Identity check" icon="id-card">
    Before the mechanic arrives, you can view their verified name, profile photo, and star rating in the app. When they reach you, check that the person matches the photo before you engage. If anything doesn't match, do not approach the vehicle and report it immediately through the app.
  </Card>
  <Card title="In-app incident reporting" icon="flag">
    After every job, you have the option to report any concerns about the mechanic's behaviour, the quality of work, or anything else that made you feel uncomfortable. Reports go directly to ZipDrop's Trust & Safety team, who review every submission and follow up where necessary.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## ZipDrop's safety policies

<Accordion title="Zero tolerance for harassment and abuse">
  ZipDrop has a strict zero-tolerance policy for harassment, threatening language, discriminatory behaviour, or any form of abuse — whether directed at a driver by a mechanic or vice versa. A single substantiated report of serious misconduct results in immediate removal from the platform. Both drivers and mechanics are subject to this policy equally.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Policy violations and account suspension">
  Any behaviour that violates ZipDrop's Community Guidelines — including fraud, misrepresentation, damage to vehicles, or refusal to follow safety procedures — can result in immediate account suspension. ZipDrop investigates all reported violations before making a final decision on permanent removal. If your account is suspended, you will receive a notification and have the opportunity to appeal.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="24/7 support team">
  ZipDrop's support team is available around the clock, every day of the year. You can reach them through the in-app help centre at any time during or after a job. Response times are typically within a few minutes for active job issues and within a few hours for general enquiries. For urgent safety concerns, use the in-app SOS button or contact **support@zipdrop.app** directly.
</Accordion>

## Insurance and liability

<Accordion title="ZipDrop platform insurance">
  ZipDrop maintains a platform-level insurance policy that covers incidents that occur during the course of an active job — from the moment a mechanic accepts your request to the moment the job is marked as complete. This policy is designed to provide an additional layer of protection in circumstances where the mechanic's own insurance may not apply. Full details of the policy coverage are available on request by emailing **support@zipdrop.app**.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Mechanic public liability insurance">
  In addition to commercial vehicle insurance, every mechanic on the platform is required to hold valid public liability insurance. This covers injury or property damage that may arise from the mechanic's work on your vehicle. ZipDrop verifies this policy as part of the onboarding process and monitors it for lapses.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Pre-existing vehicle conditions">
  ZipDrop and its mechanics are not responsible for faults, damage, or conditions that existed in your vehicle before the job began. If a mechanic identifies a pre-existing issue during the job, they will inform you and document it in the app before proceeding. This protects both you and the mechanic from disputes about the scope of work carried out.
</Accordion>

<Note>
  If you have any safety concerns before, during, or after a job — whether about a mechanic's behaviour, your personal safety, or the quality of work — contact ZipDrop directly at **support@zipdrop.app**. The Trust & Safety team reviews every report and responds to all safety-related enquiries as a priority.
</Note>

<Warning>
  If you are in immediate danger, always call **999** first. ZipDrop's SOS button connects you to emergency services, but ZipDrop's support team is not an emergency service and cannot dispatch police, ambulance, or fire services on your behalf. Once you are safe, you can contact ZipDrop support to report the incident.
</Warning>

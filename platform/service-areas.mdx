ZipDrop is growing rapidly across the UK, and you can already access on-demand roadside assistance in ten major cities and their surrounding regions. If you break down within a covered area, ZipDrop will dispatch a qualified mechanic directly to your location — whether you're on a residential street, in a car park, or pulled over on a dual carriageway. Coverage boundaries are determined by mechanic availability and demand density, so the level of service you experience may vary depending on how close you are to the centre of a covered city.

## How to check your area

You don't need to memorise coverage boundaries — ZipDrop tells you automatically when you open the app.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the ZipDrop app">
    Launch the app on your phone. ZipDrop will request permission to access your location if it hasn't already. Allow this so the app can accurately determine where you are.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Look for the coverage indicator">
    On the home screen, before you submit a request, ZipDrop displays a coverage status message based on your current GPS position. If you see **"Service available in your area"**, you're within a covered zone and can request help immediately.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check for limited coverage warnings">
    If you're near the edge of a covered area or in a zone with fewer active mechanics, you'll see a **"Coverage is limited in your area"** message. You can still submit a request, but response times may be longer than usual.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Outside coverage areas">
    If you're outside all current coverage zones, the app will let you know that ZipDrop isn't yet available at your location. You can use the in-app form to request that we prioritise your area in our next expansion round.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Rural and motorway coverage

<Accordion title="Major motorway coverage">
  ZipDrop mechanics are available on and around all major UK motorways, including the M1, M25, M6, M62, M60, M4, M5, and M8 in Scotland. If you break down on a motorway, pull onto the hard shoulder or into an emergency refuge area, then open ZipDrop to request assistance. Make sure your hazard lights are on and, where possible, stand behind the barrier while you wait.

  Response times on motorways may be longer than in urban areas due to access restrictions and the distances mechanics need to travel. ZipDrop will give you a realistic ETA based on actual mechanic locations, not an average estimate.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Remote and rural areas">
  In rural areas that fall within the broad boundary of a covered region, mechanic availability may be lower and response times significantly longer. ZipDrop will always show you an estimated arrival time before you confirm a request, so you can decide whether to wait or explore other options.

  Very remote areas — for example, isolated moorland, Highland Scotland outside of Glasgow or Edinburgh, or rural mid-Wales — are not currently covered. ZipDrop will tell you this in the app before you attempt to submit a request.
</Accordion>

<Note>
  Response times vary significantly depending on your location, the time of day, and local mechanic availability. In busy city centres during peak hours, you can typically expect a mechanic within 20–40 minutes. On a rural road late at night, it may take considerably longer. ZipDrop always shows you the live ETA before you confirm — never a generic estimate.
</Note>

## Coming soon

ZipDrop is expanding to new cities in the coming months. The following locations are actively in onboarding, with mechanics currently completing the vetting process ahead of launch:

- **Newcastle upon Tyne** and Tyne and Wear
- **Nottingham** and the East Midlands
- **Leicester** and Leicestershire
- **Southampton** and Hampshire

If you live near one of these cities and would like to be notified the moment ZipDrop launches in your area, you can sign up for launch alerts through the app under **Settings → Coverage Alerts**.

<Info>
  ZipDrop expands to new coverage areas every quarter. Each expansion involves recruiting and vetting local mechanics, completing infrastructure testing, and running a soft launch period to ensure service quality before opening to all users. Follow ZipDrop on social media or check this page for the latest updates on new city launches.
</Info>

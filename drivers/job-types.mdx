ZipDrop mechanics can help with the most common roadside emergencies — including flat tyres, dead batteries, fuel delivery, vehicle lockouts, towing, and general breakdowns. Select the service that matches your situation when you make a request, and your mechanic will arrive prepared with the right tools and equipment. If you're not sure what you need, choose **General Breakdown** and your mechanic will diagnose the issue on arrival.

## Available services

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Flat Tyre">
    If you have a flat or punctured tyre, your mechanic will assess the damage on arrival and carry out whichever repair is appropriate:

    - **Tyre change** — if you have a spare tyre (full-size or space-saver) in your boot, your mechanic will remove the flat tyre and fit the spare for you.
    - **Plug or patch repair** — if the puncture is in the central tread area and the damage is small enough, your mechanic may be able to plug or patch the tyre on the spot, so you can continue your journey on the original tyre.

    If your tyre is too damaged to repair and you don't have a spare, your mechanic will advise you on the best next step — this will typically mean arranging a tow to the nearest tyre fitting centre. You can add towing to your request at that point.

    **Before your mechanic arrives:** Check whether you have a spare tyre and a jack in your vehicle. Knowing this in advance helps your mechanic come prepared.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Battery Jump-Start">
    If your engine won't start due to a flat or drained battery, your mechanic will attempt a jump-start using either jump leads connected to their own vehicle or a portable lithium jump-start pack.

    In most cases, a successful jump-start will get you back on the road within minutes. Your mechanic will advise you to drive for at least 20–30 minutes afterwards to allow your alternator to recharge the battery fully.

    If the battery is too old or damaged to hold a charge, your mechanic will let you know. They can advise you on replacement options, and in some cases may carry a compatible replacement battery. Any parts supplied are charged separately and agreed with you before fitting.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Fuel Delivery">
    If you've run out of fuel, your mechanic will deliver up to 5 litres of the correct fuel to your location — enough to get you safely to the nearest filling station.

    **Important:** When you select Fuel Delivery in the app, you must choose the correct fuel type — **petrol** or **diesel**. Your mechanic will bring exactly what you specify. Check your fuel cap or your vehicle handbook if you're unsure.

    The 5-litre delivery is included in the flat service fee. If your vehicle requires AdBlue in addition to diesel, please mention this in your request notes — your mechanic may be able to bring this separately.

    <Warning>
      Putting the wrong fuel type in your vehicle can cause serious and expensive engine damage. Always double-check whether your car takes petrol or diesel before confirming your fuel delivery request. If you have already misfuelled, do **not** start the engine — select **General Breakdown** instead and explain the situation in your notes.
    </Warning>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Lockout Assistance">
    If you've locked your keys inside your vehicle, or your key has been lost, broken, or stolen, your mechanic can help you regain access.

    ZipDrop mechanics who carry out lockout jobs are trained in professional, non-destructive vehicle entry techniques. They use specialist tools designed to open vehicles without damaging the locks, seals, or bodywork.

    **Proof of ownership is required.** When your mechanic arrives, you'll need to show at least one of the following:

    - Vehicle Registration Certificate (V5C logbook)
    - Insurance certificate or cover note
    - Finance agreement or purchase receipt in your name

    This requirement protects you and other vehicle owners. Your mechanic may decline to proceed if satisfactory proof cannot be provided.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Towing">
    If your vehicle cannot be driven — whether due to mechanical failure, a flat tyre with no spare, or any other reason — ZipDrop can arrange recovery to a destination of your choice within the service area.

    When you select Towing, you'll be prompted to enter a destination in the app — typically a garage, dealership, or safe private address. Your mechanic will confirm the destination before loading the vehicle.

    The towing service includes:

    - Loading and securing your vehicle onto or behind the recovery vehicle
    - Transport to your chosen destination within the ZipDrop service area
    - Unloading at the destination

    If you're not sure which garage to use, your mechanic can suggest options nearby based on your vehicle's make. Long-distance recovery outside the standard service area may incur an additional charge, which will be shown in the app before you confirm.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="General Breakdown">
    If your vehicle has stopped working and you're not sure why, select General Breakdown. Your mechanic will attend, carry out a roadside diagnosis, and do their best to get you moving again.

    Common issues resolved under this service include:

    - Engine warning lights and fault codes (mechanic carries a diagnostic reader)
    - Starter motor or alternator faults
    - Overheating (where safe to assess)
    - Loose or corroded battery connections
    - Minor electrical faults

    If the issue can be fixed at the roadside, your mechanic will do so. If the vehicle needs to be taken to a workshop, your mechanic will explain what's needed and you can add towing to your job directly in the app.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

<Note>
  ZipDrop mechanics arrive equipped with a standard roadside toolkit, jump leads or a jump-start pack, a tyre plug/patch kit, basic diagnostic equipment, and safety gear. They do **not** carry spare tyres — you should check whether your own vehicle has a spare before requesting a flat tyre service. For fuel delivery, the mechanic supplies the fuel; for parts such as replacement batteries or new tyres, pricing is agreed on-site before any work begins.
</Note>

## What's not covered

ZipDrop is a roadside assistance service designed to get you moving again quickly. The following are outside the scope of what ZipDrop mechanics provide:

- **Insurance claims and accident recovery** — if your vehicle has been in a collision, contact your insurer directly. They will arrange appropriate recovery and assess the damage under your policy.
- **MOT testing** — ZipDrop mechanics do not carry out Ministry of Transport tests. Book your MOT with a registered test centre.
- **Routine servicing and maintenance** — oil changes, filter replacements, brake inspections, and scheduled servicing are not covered. Use a local garage or mobile mechanic service for planned maintenance.
- **Accident scene management** — if there are injuries or hazards at the scene of an accident, call 999 first. ZipDrop is not an emergency service.
- **Recovery outside the service area** — towing destinations must fall within ZipDrop's active service area. Check the app for your current coverage zone.

If you're unsure whether your situation is covered, open the app and select **General Breakdown** — your mechanic will assess on arrival and advise on the best course of action.

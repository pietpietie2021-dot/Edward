Your safety is ZipDrop's highest priority. Breaking down — especially in poor weather, at night, or on a busy road — can be a stressful and dangerous experience. This page walks you through the immediate steps you should take to protect yourself at the roadside, explains the safety features built into the ZipDrop app, and tells you exactly what to do if you ever feel unsafe during a job.

## At the roadside

As soon as your vehicle stops, how you position yourself and your car can make a critical difference. Work through this checklist before you do anything else:

- **Turn on your hazard lights immediately.** Do this as your first action, even before you pull over — it alerts other drivers that something is wrong.
- **Move off the road if you possibly can.** Aim for a verge, layby, slip road, or car park. Even a partial move off the carriageway significantly reduces your risk.
- **On a motorway or dual carriageway, get behind the barrier.** Leave your vehicle via the passenger side (away from traffic), take your phone and any essentials, and climb the embankment or move behind the safety barrier. Stay well away from your vehicle — a stationary car on a motorway is at serious risk of being struck.
- **Stay alert and visible.** If you have a high-visibility vest in your boot and can retrieve it safely, put it on. Keep watching for approaching traffic, especially at night or in fog.
- **Never stand between your car and oncoming traffic.** Position yourself in front of the car or behind the barrier — never in the gap between your vehicle and the live lane.

<Warning>
  If you break down on a motorway, do not remain in or beside your vehicle. Always exit via the left-hand door, move to the far side of the barrier, and wait there for your mechanic and emergency services. Follow the latest guidance from National Highways (formerly Highways England) at all times: [nationalhighways.co.uk](https://nationalhighways.co.uk).
</Warning>

## ZipDrop safety features

ZipDrop is built with several layers of protection so you know exactly who is coming to help you and can reach support the moment you need it.

  <Card title="Verified mechanics" icon="shield-check">
    Every mechanic on ZipDrop has passed a DBS (Disclosure and Barring Service) criminal record check and completed ZipDrop's ID verification process before they can accept jobs.
  </Card>
  <Card title="In-app chat and calling" icon="message">
    You can message or call your mechanic directly through the app. Your personal phone number is never shared — all communication is routed through ZipDrop's anonymous relay.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Live location sharing" icon="location-dot">
    Tap the **Share Trip** button on the job screen to send a live link of your location to a friend or family member. They can watch your mechanic's progress towards you in real time.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Emergency call button" icon="phone-emergency">
    A red **Emergency** button is always visible at the top of the app during an active job. One tap connects you directly to 999 (or your local emergency services number if you're abroad).
  </Card>
  <Card title="Photo ID verification" icon="id-card">
    Before your mechanic arrives, you'll see their name, photo, vehicle make, and registration plate in the app. Always check these details match the person who pulls up before you approach.
  </Card>

    ZipDrop's customer support team is on call around the clock. If anything feels wrong at any point during a job, you can reach a real person immediately.
  </Card>

## If you feel unsafe

If anything about a job makes you uncomfortable — whether it's the mechanic's behaviour, an unexpected person arriving, or simply a gut feeling — act straight away. You don't need a specific reason.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Safety Centre">
    Tap the **Shield icon** in the top-right corner of the app at any time during an active job.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Report that you feel unsafe">
    Select **'I feel unsafe'** from the menu. ZipDrop support will be notified immediately and a team member will call you within seconds.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Contact emergency services if needed">
    If you are in immediate danger, tap the red **Emergency** button to call 999 without leaving the app.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Cancel the job if you need to">
    You can cancel any job at any time — tap **Cancel Job** on the job screen. There is no penalty for cancelling on safety grounds.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  If a mechanic arrives and you don't recognise them, don't approach the vehicle. Open the ZipDrop app and check the mechanic's name, photo, and vehicle details on the job screen. If anything doesn't match — for example a different person, van, or registration plate — stay where you are, call ZipDrop support via the Shield icon, and do not allow any work to begin until the identity is confirmed.
</Note>

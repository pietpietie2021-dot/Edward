Before you can start accepting jobs on ZipDrop, your application goes through a thorough approval process. This protects drivers who rely on ZipDrop in vulnerable situations — a broken-down driver on the roadside needs to know they can trust the person showing up to help them. The process is straightforward: ZipDrop verifies your identity, qualifications, background, and vehicle, then activates your account once everything checks out.

## What ZipDrop verifies

ZipDrop reviews several pieces of information before approving your account:

<Accordion title="Identity">
  You must provide a valid, government-issued photo ID — such as a UK passport, driving licence, or national identity card. The name on your ID must match the name you registered with. ZipDrop uses this to confirm who you are and to complete the DBS check.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Right to work in the UK">
  ZipDrop is required to confirm that you have the legal right to work in the United Kingdom. Acceptable documents include a UK passport, biometric residence permit, or a share code generated via the UK Visas and Immigration online service.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="DBS enhanced background check">
  All ZipDrop mechanics and recovery professionals must pass an enhanced Disclosure and Barring Service (DBS) check. ZipDrop initiates this check on your behalf as part of the approval process — you don't need to arrange it yourself.

  The DBS check looks at criminal record history to ensure driver safety. Having a previous conviction does not automatically disqualify you; ZipDrop assesses each case individually.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Professional qualifications and certifications">
  ZipDrop verifies that the qualifications and certifications you uploaded during sign-up are valid and current. These are checked against the specialisms you selected. For example, if you selected the Towing & Recovery specialism, ZipDrop will confirm you hold the appropriate recovery operator credentials.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Vehicle insurance">
  Your vehicle insurance policy must cover **business or commercial use**. Standard personal or social domestic and pleasure (SDP) insurance policies do not cover you while attending paid callouts. ZipDrop will check that your policy explicitly includes business use before approving your account.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Vehicle roadworthiness">
  Your vehicle must hold a valid **MOT certificate** and be in a roadworthy condition. ZipDrop will check the MOT expiry date against your vehicle registration. Make sure your MOT is current before you apply — an expired MOT will prevent your application from being approved.
</Accordion>

<Warning>
  Personal vehicle insurance is **not sufficient** to work on ZipDrop. Your policy must explicitly cover business or commercial use. Contact your insurer to add business use to your policy before applying — failure to have the correct cover could invalidate your insurance and put you at legal risk.
</Warning>

<Note>
  The cost of the DBS enhanced background check is covered entirely by ZipDrop. You will not be charged for this at any point during the application process.
</Note>

## How long does approval take?

Approval typically takes **3–5 business days** from the date your completed application is submitted. The most common cause of delays is missing or unclear documentation — so make sure everything you upload is legible, current, and complete before you submit.

## Approval stages

Your application moves through the following stages. You can check your current status at any time in the **Profile → Application Status** section of the ZipDrop Mechanic app.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Submitted">
    Your application has been received by ZipDrop. The approval team will begin reviewing your documents within one business day.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Under Review">
    Your documents and credentials are actively being verified. The DBS check is initiated at this stage. No action is required from you unless you're contacted.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Additional Information Requested (if applicable)">
    If the approval team needs clarification or further documentation, you'll receive a notification via the app and by email. Check the notification for details on exactly what's needed, then upload the required documents via **Profile → Documents**. Your application will resume review once you've responded.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Approved">
    Your account is fully activated. You'll receive a confirmation email and an in-app notification. You can now go online in the app and start accepting jobs straight away.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What if more documents are requested?

If ZipDrop needs additional documents or clarification, you'll be notified by push notification and email. To respond:

1. Open the ZipDrop Mechanic app
2. Go to **Profile → Documents**
3. Tap the document flagged as incomplete or insufficient
4. Upload the requested file or updated version
5. Tap **Resubmit**

The approval team will resume reviewing your application within one business day of your resubmission.

## What if my application is rejected?

If your application is rejected, ZipDrop will notify you by email and in the app, with a reason for the decision. Common reasons for rejection include:

- **Expired or invalid documents** — your qualifications, MOT, or insurance documents were out of date at the time of application
- **Insufficient insurance cover** — your vehicle insurance does not include business or commercial use
- **Unverifiable qualifications** — the certifications you uploaded could not be verified against a recognised accreditation body
- **DBS check result** — the enhanced DBS check returned information that prevents approval under ZipDrop's safety policy
- **Identity mismatch** — the name on your ID does not match the name on your application

If you believe your rejection was made in error, or if the issue can be resolved (for example, by updating your insurance or obtaining a renewed certificate), you can reapply. To reapply, go to **Profile → Application Status** and tap **Submit New Application**. Make sure you address the stated reason for rejection before resubmitting.

Your ZipDrop profile is the first thing drivers see when you're matched to their job — and it's how ZipDrop determines which jobs to send you. Keeping it accurate and up to date means you're matched to jobs you're qualified to handle, drivers know who to expect, and your account stays compliant with ZipDrop's document requirements. This guide covers every section of your profile and how to manage your availability.

## Profile sections

### Photo

Your profile photo is displayed to drivers when they're matched with you and throughout the job. Go to **Profile** → **Edit Photo** to upload or replace your photo. It should be a clear, professional headshot taken in good lighting — no sunglasses, filters, or cropped group photos. ZipDrop reviews submitted photos and may ask you to resubmit if the image doesn't meet the guidelines.

### Bio

Your bio is a short description shown to drivers on the job confirmation screen. It's your opportunity to give drivers a quick sense of your experience and approach. Write it in **Profile** → **Edit Bio** — you have up to **150 characters**, so keep it concise. For example: *'10 years' experience in roadside recovery and tyre fitting. Quick, reliable, and friendly.'*

### Specialisms

Your specialisms tell ZipDrop which job types to match you with. If a service type isn't listed in your specialisms, you won't be offered those jobs. Update your specialisms via **Profile** → **Specialisms** — tick each service you're qualified and equipped to carry out. If you complete additional training or acquire new equipment, update this list so you're not missing out on relevant jobs.

### Vehicle details

ZipDrop uses your vehicle information to show drivers what to look out for when you arrive. Keep this up to date via **Profile** → **My Vehicle**, especially if you change your callout vehicle. Include your make, model, colour, and registration plate.

### Documents

ZipDrop requires valid documents to keep your account active. Upload and manage them via **Profile** → **Documents**. Required documents typically include public liability insurance, any relevant trade certifications, and your driving licence. ZipDrop sends you a reminder **30 days before any document expires**, but it's good practice to check this section regularly and upload renewed documents as soon as you have them.

<Note>
  If a critical document — such as your insurance — expires and you haven't uploaded a renewed version, ZipDrop will **pause your account** until the document is updated. You won't receive job offers during this time. Upload renewals promptly to avoid any gap in your availability.
</Note>

## Setting your availability zone

Your availability zone controls how far from your current location ZipDrop will match you to jobs.

- **Default radius:** ZipDrop matches you within a **15-mile radius** of your location at the time of each job offer.
- **Custom radius:** Drag the radius slider in **Profile** → **Availability Zone** to set anywhere between **5 and 30 miles**.

Your zone can be adjusted at any time — changes take effect immediately for new job offers.

<Tip>
  If you're having a quiet period and jobs aren't coming through, try **widening your radius temporarily**. Expanding from 15 to 25 or 30 miles significantly increases the pool of jobs you're eligible for, particularly in rural or lower-density areas.
</Tip>

## Pause notifications

If you need a short break without going fully offline, you can pause job alerts without changing your availability status. Go to **Profile** → **Pause Alerts** and select a duration:

- 30 minutes
- 1 hour
- 2 hours

During a pause, you won't receive any job notifications. Your status returns to active automatically when the pause period ends. This is useful if you're on a break, in an area with no signal, or dealing with something that means you can't accept jobs for a short window.

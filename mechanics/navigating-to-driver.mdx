Once you've accepted a job, your priority is getting to the driver safely and efficiently. ZipDrop provides everything you need to find the driver — an in-app map with turn-by-turn navigation, the driver's vehicle details, and direct communication tools so you can confirm the exact location before you arrive. A faster response time means a better experience for the driver and a stronger rating for you.

## How navigation works

After you confirm you're on your way, the ZipDrop Mechanic app opens an in-app map with a route plotted to the driver's pinned location. The map provides **turn-by-turn directions** using your device's GPS. Directions are spoken aloud so you can keep your eyes on the road.

If you prefer to use a different navigation app, you can open the route directly in:
- **Google Maps** — tap the **Google Maps** icon on the navigation screen
- **Apple Maps** — tap the **Apple Maps** icon (iOS only)

The driver's pin is transferred automatically, so you don't need to manually enter the destination.

## Viewing the driver's details en route

While you're navigating, you can access the driver's details at any time by swiping up on the job panel at the bottom of the screen. This shows you:

- **Driver's first name**
- **Vehicle make, model, and colour** — so you can visually identify the vehicle when you arrive
- **Vehicle registration plate** — always confirm this matches before you start any work
- **Notes from the driver** — the driver may have added useful information, such as a more specific location description, gate codes, or details about the breakdown

## Contacting the driver en route

If you need to check in with the driver before you arrive, you have two options directly from the job screen:

**In-app chat** — tap the **chat icon** to send a text message to the driver through ZipDrop's messaging system. This is the recommended way to confirm a precise location, especially if the driver has broken down somewhere with a vague pin — for example, on a motorway slip road or inside a multi-storey car park.

**Phone call** — tap the **phone icon** to call the driver directly through ZipDrop. Your personal phone number is never shared with the driver; the call is routed anonymously through ZipDrop's platform.

## If the driver's location is inaccurate

GPS pins are not always precise, particularly in areas with poor signal, in tunnels, or in dense urban environments. If you arrive at the pinned location and the driver isn't there, don't panic. Here's what to do:

1. **Message the driver** via the in-app chat and ask them to confirm their exact location or drop an updated pin in their ZipDrop Driver app
2. **Call the driver** using the in-app phone if a faster response is needed
3. **Look for visual cues** — the driver's vehicle details are on your screen; scan the immediate area for a vehicle matching that description
4. If you've been unable to locate the driver after a reasonable effort, contact **ZipDrop Support** via **Help → Contact Support**

## Arriving on site

When you pull up at the driver's location, tap **I've Arrived** in the app. This does two things:

1. **Notifies the driver** that you're on site, so they know to look out for you
2. **Starts the job timer**, which logs the start time for the callout

<Tip>
  Before you start any work, walk up to the vehicle and **verify the registration plate** matches what's shown in the job details. This protects you and the driver, and ensures you're working on the right vehicle.
</Tip>

<Warning>
  Always park **safely and legally** at the breakdown location. If you're attending a breakdown on a busy road, a motorway, or a slip road, follow Highway Code guidance: use hazard lights, wear a high-visibility vest, and keep yourself and the driver away from live traffic at all times. Your safety comes first.
</Warning>

Every time you complete a job through ZipDrop, your earnings are calculated automatically based on the type of service you provided, any parts you supplied, the distance you travelled, and whether the job fell during a peak demand period. ZipDrop deducts a 20% service fee from the job total before paying out, covering platform maintenance, customer support, and payment processing — so the figures below reflect what you receive after that deduction.

## Earnings breakdown per job

### Base rates by service type

Your base earnings depend on the type of job you accept. These ranges reflect typical payouts after the ZipDrop service fee has been applied:

| Service | Mechanic Base Earnings |
|---|---|
| Flat Tyre | £25–£45 |
| Battery Jump | £20–£35 |
| Fuel Delivery | £18–£28 |
| Lockout | £35–£65 |
| Towing | £55–£120 |
| General Breakdown | £30–£55 |

### Parts reimbursement

If you supply parts to complete a job — such as fuel for a delivery, a replacement tyre, or jump leads — ZipDrop reimburses the full cost of those parts on top of your base rate. You log parts used during or immediately after the job in the app, and the cost is added to your job total before any fee calculation.

### Distance bonus

For jobs where the driver's location is more than 5 miles from your current position at the time of job acceptance, you earn an additional **£0.50 per mile** for the full distance travelled. This is calculated automatically using the route shown in the app and added to your job total.

### ZipDrop service fee

ZipDrop deducts a **20% service fee** from the gross job total (base rate + parts + distance bonus) before calculating your payout. This fee is shown clearly in your post-job earnings summary so you always know exactly how it was applied.

## Viewing your earnings

Open the **Earnings** tab in your ZipDrop app to see a full breakdown of what you've made. The tab displays three views:

- **Today** — all jobs completed since midnight, with individual amounts
- **This week** — Monday-to-date, including any peak multipliers applied
- **All time** — your cumulative earnings since joining ZipDrop

Tap any individual job in the list to see a line-by-line breakdown showing the base rate, parts reimbursement, distance bonus, peak multiplier (if applicable), and the service fee deduction.

## Earnings during peak hours

During periods of high driver demand, ZipDrop applies a **peak hour multiplier** to your base rate. Multipliers range from **1.2x to 1.5x** depending on demand intensity in your area. When a multiplier is active, a banner appears at the top of your home screen in the app showing the current rate and how long it is expected to last.

The multiplier applies to your base rate only — parts reimbursement and distance bonuses are unaffected. The ZipDrop service fee is applied to the full multiplied total.

<Tip>
  The highest multipliers typically appear during the **morning rush (7–9am)**, the **evening commute (5–8pm)**, and **late nights from 10pm onwards**, particularly on Fridays and Saturdays. Switching yourself to available during these windows is one of the most effective ways to increase your hourly earnings without taking on extra jobs.
</Tip>

<Note>
  All earnings figures shown in the app during and immediately after a job are estimates until the job is fully confirmed and closed. Final amounts are locked in once both you and the driver have confirmed job completion. If there is a discrepancy, contact ZipDrop support via the app within 48 hours of the job.
</Note>

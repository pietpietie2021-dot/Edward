Your rating is one of the most important numbers on your ZipDrop profile. It directly influences how often you're matched to jobs, whether you qualify for the Top Rated badge and ZipDrop Pro status, and how drivers choose between available mechanics. A strong rating builds trust with drivers before you've even arrived on the scene — and a declining one can limit your access to the platform. Understanding how it works gives you the best chance of keeping it high.

## How your rating is calculated

Your rating is a **rolling average of your last 100 completed jobs**. If you've completed fewer than 100 jobs, it's calculated across all of them. Every new job rating that comes in replaces the oldest one in the window, which means a single poor review has less impact as your job count grows — but it also means consistent quality matters more than a single great performance.

Your current rating is displayed on your profile page and in the **Earnings dashboard**.

---

## What drivers rate

After every completed job, drivers are prompted to rate their experience. They provide:

- A **1–5 star rating**
- Optional **written feedback** — a free-text comment about their experience
- **Quick tags** — pre-set descriptors such as *'arrived on time'*, *'friendly'*, *'fixed the problem quickly'*, or *'kept me informed'*

All three are visible to you in **Profile** → **My Ratings**.

---

## Factors that help your rating

While every driver is different, the mechanics who consistently earn high ratings tend to share the same habits:

- **Arriving within or before the estimated ETA** — drivers in a breakdown situation are stressed and time-sensitive. Showing up on time, or earlier than expected, makes a strong first impression.
- **Communicating clearly before and during the job** — use the in-app messaging to let the driver know you're on the way, your estimated arrival, and any updates if you're delayed.
- **Completing the job correctly the first time** — a clean, lasting fix is what drivers remember. If a job needs more work than expected, explain it clearly before proceeding.
- **Being professional and courteous** — how you treat people stays with them. A calm, reassuring manner when a driver is stressed makes a meaningful difference to how they rate you.

## What happens if your rating drops?

ZipDrop monitors mechanic ratings continuously and takes action at two thresholds:

**Below 4.0**
You'll receive a warning notification in the app, and a member of the ZipDrop support team will contact you with tailored advice on what's driving the decline and how to address it.

**Below 3.5**
Your account will be temporarily suspended and you will be required to complete a **mandatory review call** with ZipDrop before being reinstated. During the call, ZipDrop will discuss the feedback you've received and agree a clear action plan with you.

**Persistent issues**
If your rating continues to decline after multiple warnings and support interventions, ZipDrop may terminate your account. This is always a last resort, and you'll receive written notice before any final decision is made.

---

## Disputing a rating

If you believe a rating was unfair — for example, if a driver left a negative review for circumstances clearly outside your control, such as a parts delay or road closure — you can flag it for review. Go to **Profile** → **My Ratings**, find the relevant review, and tap **Flag Review**. Add a brief explanation of the circumstances and submit.

ZipDrop's team reviews flagged ratings against job data, including GPS logs, in-app messages, and job notes. Flagging a review does not guarantee removal, but it does ensure a human reviews it fairly.

<Note>
  ZipDrop does not remove negative reviews simply because you disagree with them. Reviews are only removed if they violate ZipDrop's content guidelines — for example, if they contain abusive language, are factually false, or relate to a job that was subsequently disputed and resolved in your favour.
</Note>

<Tip>
  After every job, take a moment to **read any written feedback** the driver left, even if your star rating was high. Positive tags and comments tell you what to keep doing. Constructive criticism — even when it stings — is the fastest way to identify a habit or gap you might not have noticed yourself.
</Tip>

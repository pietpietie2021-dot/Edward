Once your account is approved, earning on ZipDrop is straightforward: go online in the app, and ZipDrop will start sending you job alerts for breakdown requests near your current location. You stay in control at every step — you can see the job type, estimated earnings, and distance before you commit, and you're never obligated to accept a job you can't safely or practically carry out.

## Going online

To start receiving job requests, open the ZipDrop Mechanic app and toggle your status to **Available**. ZipDrop uses your live GPS location to match you with drivers who have broken down nearby. Make sure location services are enabled for the app at all times while you're online — without GPS access, ZipDrop can't match you accurately.

<Tip>
  Keep your phone's volume turned up and notifications enabled for the ZipDrop Mechanic app. Job alerts play an audible sound and appear on your lock screen — if your phone is on silent or notifications are blocked, you may miss requests before the 30-second acceptance window closes.
</Tip>

## How job matching works

ZipDrop's algorithm considers several factors when sending you job requests: your current location, the specialisms you're registered for, and your proximity to the stranded driver. You'll only receive job requests that match your listed specialisms — for example, if you're registered for tyre and battery jobs but not towing, you won't receive recovery requests.

## What a job card shows you

When a job request comes in, a **job card** pops up on your screen showing:

- **Service type** — e.g. flat tyre, dead battery, lockout
- **Distance to driver** — how far away the driver is from your current location
- **Estimated earnings** — your take for completing the job, shown before you accept
- **Time to accept** — you have **30 seconds** to decide; the countdown is shown on the card

## Accepting a job

<Steps>
  <Step title="Review the incoming job card">
    When a job request arrives, the job card appears on your screen automatically. Take a moment to check the service type, estimated earnings, and how far the driver is from your current location.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check the job type and distance">
    Make sure the job is something you're equipped to handle and that the distance is practical. Factor in current traffic conditions — the distance shown is the straight-line distance, not drive time.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Tap 'Accept' within 30 seconds">
    Tap **Accept** to take the job. If the 30-second timer runs out before you respond, the job is automatically passed to the next available mechanic in the area. Once you accept, the job is assigned to you.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm you can reach the driver">
    After accepting, you'll see a confirmation screen with the driver's location and a prompt to confirm you're on your way. Tap **I'm on my way** to notify the driver that help is coming.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Navigation starts automatically">
    The in-app map loads with turn-by-turn directions to the driver's pinned location. You can also open the route in Google Maps or Apple Maps if you prefer. See [Navigating to the Driver](/mechanics/navigating-to-driver) for full details.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Declining a job

You can decline any job request without penalty — simply tap **Decline** on the job card before the timer runs out. You don't need to provide a reason. Declining occasionally is completely normal and won't affect your account standing.

However, if your decline rate becomes consistently high, ZipDrop may reduce your visibility in the job-matching algorithm — meaning you'll receive fewer job requests. If you're going through a period where you can't take jobs, it's better to go **Offline** rather than staying online and repeatedly declining.

## Job queue

You can only hold **one active job at a time**. Once you accept a job, you won't receive new job requests until that job is marked as complete or cancelled. This ensures you can give your full attention to the driver you're currently helping.

If you accept a job and then find you're unable to attend — for example, due to a sudden emergency — contact ZipDrop Support immediately via **Help → Contact Support** in the app. Don't leave a driver without assistance without letting ZipDrop know.

## Going offline

When you're done for the day, or need a break, toggle your status to **Unavailable** in the app. You'll immediately stop receiving job requests. Going offline does not affect your account in any way — you can go back online whenever you're ready.

If you have an active job when you tap **Unavailable**, you'll remain assigned to that job until it's completed or resolved. The offline toggle only prevents new job requests from coming in.

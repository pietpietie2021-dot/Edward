Finishing a job well is what earns you strong ratings, repeat business, and consistent income on ZipDrop. Once the repair or service is done, the completion process is quick: log any parts used, get the driver to confirm in their app, and payment is processed automatically. No invoicing, no chasing — ZipDrop handles the financial side so you can focus on the work.

<Tip>
  Before starting any work, always verify the driver's identity and confirm the vehicle registration matches the details on your job card. This protects you in the event of any dispute and ensures you're working on the correct vehicle.
</Tip>

## How to complete a job

<Steps>
  <Step title="Finish the repair or service">
    Complete the work to the best of your ability. If you used any parts — such as a replacement tyre, a new battery, or a can of fuel — keep these to hand as you'll need to log them in the next step.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Tap 'Job Complete' in the app">
    Once the work is done, tap **Job Complete** on the active job screen. This signals to ZipDrop that the service has been carried out and starts the payment process.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Log any parts used">
    If you supplied parts or materials during the job, you'll be prompted to select or enter them. Tap **Add Part** and choose from the list or enter a custom description. The driver will see the updated total in their app before they confirm — ZipDrop's pricing is transparent, so there are no surprises.

    If no parts were used, skip this step by tapping **No parts used**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Driver confirms completion">
    The driver receives a prompt in their ZipDrop app to confirm the job is complete and the updated price (if parts were added). Once they tap **Confirm**, the job is marked as closed and payment is triggered automatically.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Payment is processed">
    ZipDrop processes the payment instantly upon driver confirmation. Your earnings appear in your **Earnings dashboard** within 30 minutes. You'll also receive a push notification confirming the amount.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Rate the driver">
    After the job closes, you'll be prompted to leave a star rating (1–5) for the driver. Your ratings help ZipDrop maintain quality across the platform — be honest and fair. Drivers are also asked to rate you, and your average rating is visible to drivers when you accept their job request.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Your earnings appear in the **Earnings** section of the ZipDrop Mechanic app within **30 minutes** of job completion. From there, you can transfer your balance to your linked bank account at any time. Standard bank transfer times apply once a payout is initiated.
</Note>

## Job notes and photos

Before you start work and after you finish, it's strongly recommended that you photograph the vehicle. Use the in-app camera via **Job → Add Photos** to capture:

- The vehicle's condition on arrival, including any pre-existing damage
- The completed work (e.g. the new tyre fitted, the battery installed)
- The registration plate, confirming it matches your job card

Photos are stored automatically in your **Job History** and can be invaluable if a driver raises a dispute about the condition of their vehicle or the quality of the work. You can access past job records at any time via **History** in the app menu.

## If you can't resolve the issue

Sometimes a breakdown turns out to be more serious than the original call type suggested. If you're unable to complete the job — for example, the driver called for a jump-start but the vehicle has a more significant fault that can't be resolved roadside — here's what to do:

1. Explain the situation clearly to the driver
2. In the app, tap **Unable to complete — additional service needed**
3. Select the reason from the list (e.g. vehicle requires towing, fault beyond roadside repair)
4. Confirm by tapping **Submit**

ZipDrop will automatically offer the driver the next appropriate service type — such as a tow to a garage. **You will still be paid for your callout**, even if the original repair couldn't be completed. The callout fee reflects your time and travel, regardless of the outcome.

<Accordion title="What counts as an 'unable to complete' situation?">
  Common scenarios where you should select **Unable to complete** include:

  - You attempted a jump-start but the battery will not hold charge and the vehicle cannot be driven safely
  - A tyre is damaged beyond a simple change (e.g. the wheel or suspension is also damaged)
  - The vehicle requires specialist diagnostic equipment not available at the roadside
  - The driver's keys are damaged and lockout assistance requires a specialist locksmith
  - The breakdown location presents a safety risk that prevents you from carrying out the work safely

  If you're ever unsure whether to attempt a repair or call it as 'unable to complete', err on the side of caution and contact **ZipDrop Support** for guidance.
</Accordion>
